# Team Culture Guide

## Our Values in Action

### Psychological Safety

We believe everyone should feel safe to speak up, ask questions, and make mistakes. If you are afraid to ask a "dumb question," that is a signal we need to fix our culture, not a signal about you.

**How we practice it:**
- Managers share their own mistakes in team meetings
- We celebrate "learning moments" in retrospectives — what went wrong and what we learned
- No question is too basic in `#engineering-help`
- We do not interrupt people in meetings. If you notice someone being talked over, say "I'd like to hear what [name] was saying."

**If you feel psychologically unsafe:** Talk to your manager, your mentor, or HR. You can also use the anonymous feedback form linked in the HR portal.

### Feedback Culture

We give feedback early and often. Waiting for a performance review to share important feedback is a failure of leadership.

**How to give good feedback:**
- **Be specific:** "In the PR review yesterday, the comment about the database query was unclear because it didn't explain what the alternative should be" — not "your reviews are confusing"
- **Be timely:** Give feedback within 24-48 hours of the event while it's fresh
- **Be kind:** Assume positive intent; focus on behavior and impact, not character
- **Be direct:** Softening feedback so much that the message is lost is not kindness — it's a disservice

**How to receive feedback:**
- Say "thank you" first, even if you disagree
- Ask clarifying questions before defending yourself
- Take time to reflect before responding if the feedback is surprising
- It's okay to disagree — explain your perspective calmly and be open to being wrong

### Work-Life Integration

We work hard and we rest. Sustainable pace is a feature, not a bug.

- If you are regularly working more than 45 hours a week, talk to your manager — something is wrong with our planning, not with you
- We do not expect responses to Slack messages outside of working hours
- Take your vacation. Unused vacation is a sign of poor planning, not dedication
- If you are burning out, say so. We would rather adjust scope than lose a great person

## Belonging and Inclusion

### Employee Resource Groups

- **Women in Tech** — Monthly meetups, mentorship program, speaker series
- **LGBTQ+ Alliance** — Safe space, advocacy, events, allyship resources
- **BIPOC in Tech** — Community, mentorship, career development, sponsorship program
- **Parents & Caregivers** — Flexible work support, community, parental leave guidance
- **Neurodiversity Network** — Support and advocacy for neurodivergent employees

All ERGs are open to allies. Join the Slack channel for any ERG to get involved.

### Mentorship Program

Every new employee is matched with a mentor **outside their direct team** for the first 90 days. Your mentor is a resource for:
- Navigating company culture and unwritten rules
- Career advice and growth planning
- Introductions to people across the company
- A safe space to ask questions you might not ask your manager

Contact hr@acme.com if you have not been matched within your first week.

### Inclusive Meeting Practices

- Share agendas 24 hours in advance so people can prepare
- Start meetings on time and end on time
- Rotate who takes notes — don't always ask the same person
- Explicitly invite quieter voices: "We haven't heard from [name] yet — what do you think?"
- Use closed captions for video calls (enabled by default in Zoom)

## Recognition

We recognize contributions publicly and privately:

- **Shoutouts** in `#kudos` Slack channel — anyone can post, anytime
- **Spot bonuses** — managers can award $100-$500 for exceptional contributions; no approval needed up to $250
- **Quarterly awards** — team-nominated, company-wide recognition with a cash prize
- **Promotion** — we promote from within whenever possible; talk to your manager about your growth path

**Do not wait to be recognized — recognize others.** A culture of appreciation starts with each of us. If someone helped you, tell them and tell the team.

## Conflict Resolution

Disagreements are healthy. Unresolved conflicts are not.

### Step 1: Talk directly

Most conflicts can be resolved with a direct, private conversation. Assume the other person has good intentions and approach the conversation with curiosity, not accusation.

### Step 2: Involve your manager

If a direct conversation doesn't resolve the issue, bring it to your manager. They can help mediate or provide perspective.

### Step 3: HR

For serious issues — harassment, discrimination, or anything that makes you feel unsafe — contact HR directly at hr@acme.com or use the anonymous reporting form. HR will investigate confidentially.

## Remote Work

We are a hybrid team. Most people are in the office 2-3 days per week, but remote work is fully supported.

**Remote best practices:**
- Keep your camera on during team meetings when possible — it builds connection
- Over-communicate your status: if you're heads-down, set a Slack status
- Block focus time on your calendar so people know when you're available
- If you're in a different timezone, agree with your manager on core overlap hours

**Home office stipend:** New employees receive a $500 home office stipend in their first month. Submit receipts through the HR portal.
